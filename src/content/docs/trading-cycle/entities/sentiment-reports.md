Sentiment reports provide short-lived external context.

## What A Sentiment Report Contains

A sentiment report can include:

- current headline pressure
- institutional or analyst bias
- macro and event risk
- positioning or flow-style context
- an expiry window so the operator knows when it is getting stale

## Why It Matters

The chart cannot tell the whole story when the market is reacting to events, policy language, or other macro drivers.

Sentiment reports give Cortiq a separate place to hold that context.

## Best Use Cases

Use sentiment reports when:

- the symbol is especially sensitive to macro events
- headline risk matters
- you want external context to support or challenge the technical setup

## Important Distinction

Sentiment should guide confidence and caution. It should not override the full trading framework by itself.
